# Pelvic Floor Therapy

Pelvic floor therapy helps with recovery after pregnancy and birth.
It is not only for people who had complications.

Many postpartum symptoms are common — but not something you have to live with.

---

## What Pelvic Floor Therapy Helps With

Pelvic floor therapy can help with:

- Pain during or after sex
- Leaking urine or stool
- Pelvic pain or pressure
- Core weakness or separation (diastasis recti)
- Pain after vaginal birth or C-section
- Feeling “not connected” to your core

---

## When to Consider Pelvic Floor Therapy

Consider pelvic floor therapy if:

- You’re postpartum and still uncomfortable
- You were never assessed after birth
- You were told symptoms were “normal” but they persist
- You want support returning to movement or exercise

---

## How to Find a Pelvic Floor Therapist

Start here:

- [APTA Pelvic Health Physical Therapist Directory](https://aptapelvichealth.org/find-a-pt/)

Search tips:

- Look for “pelvic health” or “women’s health”
- Ask if they treat postpartum and post-C-section patients
- Ask if they have experience with postpartum care
